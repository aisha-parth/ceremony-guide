Baraat

Parth’s vibrant dance and high-energy procession will set the tone as his family shows off their best moves on the way to the wedding. But why should Aisha miss out on the celebration? She’ll have her own joyful baraat, dancing alongside her family and friends to lively tunes, building excitement as they make their way to the festivities.

Pokhwanu

As Parth’s procession arrives at the gate, the bride’s mother will warmly welcome the groom’s family. She will apply a tilak to his forehead and playfully attempt to catch his nose—a lighthearted tradition symbolizing Parth’s ability to overcome any challenges the couple may face together.

Ganesh Puja

Prayers will be offered to Lord Ganesh, seeking his blessings to remove all obstacles from the couple’s path. In Hindu tradition, Lord Ganesh is always worshipped first before embarking on any new chapter in life.

Vaar Puja

Aisha’s parents honor the groom by cleansing his feet with honey and milk, then offering garlands, sweets, and prayers—symbolizing respect and warmly welcoming Parth into the family.

Kanya Aagman

Aisha is accompanied by Kishan, her uncles, and her aunts who will be holding a “phoolon ki chaadar”. As Aisha enters the mandap, they are separated by an Antarpat (cloth) which signifies that they come as separate individuals.

Kanyadaan & Hasta Milap

Aisha’s parents, with the blessings of Swaminarayan Bhagwan, will place the right hand in Parth’s right hand, thereby entrusting her happiness to him from now on. Parth’s scarf is then tied to Aisha’s veil, signifying the union of their souls.

Agni Puja & Mangal Pheras

Parth and Aisha walk around the holy fire four times, symbolizing core aspirations of married life- Dharma (Duty & Religion), Artha (Prosperity), Kama (Family) and Moksha (Salvation). In each phera, Parth receives a gift from all of Aisha’s brothers.

Saptapadi

Parth and Aisha will take seven sacred steps and recite the following vows to another:

  1. To keep a pure household, avoiding all things harmful to our health.
  2. To develop mental, physical, and spiritual strength.
  3. To acquire wealth by righteous means.
  4. To acquire knowledge, happiness, and harmony by mutual love and trust.
  5. To pray for virtuous, intelligent, and courageous children.
  6. To care for each other through sickness and health.
  7. To vow to always remain true companions and life long partners.

Mangalsutra & Sindoor

Parth will place the mangalsutra around Aisha’s neck and apply sindoor along her hair parting. The mangalsutra and sindoor symbolize the groom’s lifelong protection of the bride and his promise of commitment, mutual respect, and eternal love.

Kansar Bhojan

Parth and Aisha will share their first meal together, feeding each other sweets—symbolizing their promise to share and nurture everything in life.

Akhand Saubhagyavati

Aisha will receive blessings from married women, who will gently whisper their secrets to everlasting marital bliss.

Aashirwad & Vidai

Parth and Aisha are officially ‘man and wife’! The joyful couple will seek blessings from God, the priest, their parents, and all their loved ones. Aisha will share heartfelt goodbyes with her family as she prepares to begin her new life with Parth.
